Project Overview

Led by TEMDEC at Kyushu University Hospital, this groundbreaking initiative aims to build sustainable capacity for telemedicine in developing countries through systematic training of hospital engineers and collaboration with research and education networks (RENs). Our comprehensive approach includes Train-the-Trainer programs, hands-on technical training, and domestic workshops across Asia, enabling over 30 hospitals to join international telemedicine networks and significantly improving regional healthcare connectivity.

Key Activities

Train-the-Trainer Program

Intensive 1-month hands-on training at TEMDEC, Japan, covering DVTS, H.323, Vidyo systems, and medical equipment integration.

APAN On-the-Job Training

Real-time support during medical sessions, learning system setup, live troubleshooting, and network integration with global experts.

Philippines Workshop (Cebu)

16 participants from 10+ hospitals learned basic system setup and A/V integration in November 2017.

Indonesia Workshop (Jakarta)

40+ participants from 22 institutions trained on REN-based systems and collaborative testing in October 2017.

Asia Telemedicine Symposium

Annual gathering to review progress, discuss new technologies, and promote international partnerships.

Challenges Addressed

Rural Staff Shortage

Remote hospitals often lacked engineers, requiring repeated training efforts and localized support.

Network Infrastructure

Weak REN coverage in some areas hindered real-time sessions and reduced training participation.

Equipment Compatibility

Many hospitals used outdated A/V systems, affecting session quality and requiring investment.

Language Barriers

Multinational sessions faced communication challenges, reducing effective collaboration.
